Intro

Hi, I’m Raphael Ugboko, a PhD researcher in Human-Centered Computing at Clemson University and an aspiring Product Manager passionate about designing innovative solutions that meet user needs while driving business impact. From developing assistive training technologies for athletes with disabilities to evaluating privacy vulnerabilities in autonomous vehicle systems, I thrive at the intersection of technology, user experience, and impactful problem-solving.

I was a Product Manager at KPMG for 2 years+ before grad school and I managed a $1000m+ core banking system implementation project. I also have some years of experience in managing high stakes Civil Engineering projects through my internships in college. So I have practical, real-life experience. I also gained some hands-on experience in product management through class projects and collaborations with organizations like the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), and the Virtual Prototyping of Autonomy-Enabled Ground Systems (ViPR-GS) projects. These experiences have honed my ability to:

  • Analyze customer needs to define and prioritize features.
  • Develop product roadmaps that align with stakeholder goals.
  • Work cross-functionally with engineering, design, and research teams to deliver user-focused solutions.

I’m driven by a belief that great products start with a deep understanding of people. Outside of work, I am a huge fitness nerd and I am also the biggest Classical music fan. In my free time, I like to read books, bake, and make plans on my vision board.
